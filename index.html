The problem

Enterprise buyers consistently overpay their vendors

Procurement teams are stretched thin. Vendor negotiations are time-consuming, emotionally charged, and require market data no one has. The result: money left on the table every renewal cycle.

01
⏳

Renewals are low priority

Finance and procurement teams are pulled in too many directions to dedicate real time to each vendor renewal — so they default to accepting the vendor's opening offer.

02
📊

No benchmark data

Buyers negotiate blind. They rarely know what a comparable company paid for the same tool — which is exactly the leverage vendors rely on.

03
🔁

Negotiation is uncomfortable

Most people aren't trained negotiators. When a vendor pushes back, buyers tend to concede rather than hold their position — even when the data is on their side.

How it works

From contract intake to closed deal

Inferix handles the full negotiation lifecycle autonomously, with a human in the loop at every critical decision point.

1

Connect your stack

Sync your vendor contracts, renewal dates, invoices, and usage data via native integrations. Inferix builds a full picture of your spend in minutes.

2

Strategy is generated

Inferix analyses benchmark pricing, your actual usage, and vendor behaviour patterns to generate a negotiation strategy with a target price and walk-away floor.

3

Inferix negotiates

The AI takes the wheel — sending emails, responding to counteroffers, and adjusting its position in real time. You're notified when human judgement is needed.

4

Deal closed, logged

Once a deal is agreed, Inferix logs the outcome and uses it to sharpen its benchmark model across your entire vendor portfolio.

FAQ

Questions we actually get asked

Does Inferix send emails on my behalf without me seeing them?
Not unless you tell it to. By default, every email Inferix drafts goes to your approval queue before sending. You can graduate to full autonomy for specific vendor types or spend thresholds — that's entirely your call, configured in your account settings.
What happens if a negotiation goes badly?
Inferix is designed with walk-away floors you set yourself. If a vendor won't move past your defined threshold, Inferix flags the deal for human review rather than accepting unfavourable terms. It won't close a deal you wouldn't approve.
How does Inferix know what a fair price is?
Inferix builds its benchmark pricing model from anonymised data across all closed deals on the platform. The more deals it processes, the sharper its benchmarks get. In the early phase, our team also supplements this with curated market research and vendor pricing intelligence.
Can vendors tell they're talking to an AI?
Inferix communicates professionally and in plain language — indistinguishable from a well-prepared procurement contact. We don't take a position on disclosure; that's a decision for your organisation to make based on your vendor relationships and applicable regulations.
What integrations are available right now?
Currently in development: Google Workspace (Gmail + Drive), Microsoft 365 (Outlook), and Slack for notifications. Direct integrations with contract management tools (Ironclad, Docusign) and ERP systems are on the roadmap. We're also offering custom integration support for early enterprise customers.
We're early stage — is Inferix right for us?
If you're spending meaningfully on SaaS tools and renewing contracts, Inferix can add value regardless of company size. Our Starter tier is specifically designed for smaller teams who want professional procurement without the headcount. Reach out and we'll be honest about whether you're the right fit.
